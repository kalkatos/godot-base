# GAME CONCEPT: Street

## 1. Game Identity

- Working title: Street
- Genre: action roguelite
- Perspective: 2D side-scrolling
- Player count: single-player
- Target platform: PC

## 2. Elevator Pitch

Vesper is a side-scrolling action roguelite where the player fights through waves of enemies by chaining martial arts combos and unlocking new techniques after each level-up. Every run turns the fighter into a different kind of bruiser as new techniques reshape the combat rhythm.

## 3. Player Fantasy

Feel like a stylish street fighter who can turn a crowd into a rhythm of broken bones, rising power, and cinematic finishers.

## 4. Core Mechanics

- Move through a side-scrolling arena while managing enemy pressure and positioning.
- Execute light, heavy, and special attacks with precise timing to build combo chains.
- Spend experience to unlock techniques that change movement, range, or damage patterns.
- Trigger temporary power surges to break enemy formations and survive burst waves.

## 5. Core Loop

- Enter a stage and clear a wave of enemies while keeping the combo chain alive.
- Gain experience, level up, and choose a new technique that changes the next fight.
- Use the new technique to break the next wave faster and push deeper into the run.

## 6. Unique Features

- Level-up techniques are true fighting-game moves, not passive stat boosts.
- Each technique can branch into different combo routes, creating build variety from one run to the next.
- The game treats crowd pressure as a rhythm puzzle rather than a simple numbers problem.

## 7. References

- From Street Fighter, we borrow precise movement, combo readability, and the feel of martial arts mastery.
- From Vampire Survivors, we borrow wave-based survival progression and short, replayable runs.
- From Scott Pilgrim, we borrow stylish over-the-top combat energy and clear, satisfying hit feedback.

## 8. Further Notes

- The combat should stay readable at a glance, with strong hit reactions and clear enemy roles.
- The level-up system should emphasize meaningful technique choices over simple stat inflation.
